My Time at the rOpenSci Unconf

Jun 5, 2018 1 min read

I thought I’d summarize my time at the rOpenSci unconf. Overall, I had a really great time and I commend the rOpenSci crew for putting together an event that was really welcoming.

I proposed building a Synthetic Data Generation framework, based on some previous work I’ve done with Bayesian Networks. In retrospect, I think I should have had a more clear plan for showing people how it worked - maybe we could have just installed the current package and had team members generate small datasets. I only had a vague idea of how to perhaps parametrize the network, which seemed to lose people even further.

At this point, I think I had a bit of a brain meltdown and I decided to bow out of this framework and help the flow cytometry crew (Sean Hughes, Ju Yeong Kim, Malisa Smith, and Angela Li) with the umapr package.
